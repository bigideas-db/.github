## What is BigIdeasDB?

> **BigIdeasDB is an AI-powered platform that surfaces validated startup ideas from over 1 million real user complaints across 10 platforms** — Reddit, G2, Capterra, Upwork, the Apple App Store, and Google Play — then layers in verified revenue data, acquisition listings, and VC funding intelligence so every opportunity comes with proof of demand.

Most startups fail because they build something nobody wants. Market research is slow, expensive, and usually just guesswork. Founders burn weeks scrolling Reddit and reading reviews, only to discover too late that nobody was ever going to pay.

BigIdeasDB removes the guesswork. It surfaces **real problems people are already complaining about online**, scores them by frequency and severity, backs them with **verified revenue signals**, and then helps you validate, plan, and ship — all in one place with **40+ databases, pipelines, and AI tools.**

## 🗂️ Data Sources

BigIdeasDB pulls real complaints and demand signals from **10 platforms**. Every opportunity is backed by evidence with a direct link to the original source.

### Reddit
The largest source of unfiltered user pain on the internet. BigIdeasDB analyzes discussions, complaints, and feature requests across **3M+ subreddits** to surface problems people are actively asking someone to solve — every problem links back to the original thread. 📺 [Reddit Pipeline tutorial](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=IC7LYZqLezY)

### G2
**850K+ negative reviews** across **350+ software categories** and **8,000+ companies** reveal exactly what users hate about existing tools — missing features, broken workflows, pricing complaints — scored by gap size. 📺 [G2 Analysis tutorial](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fQk0Dcy2WFY)

### Capterra
Business-software reviews expose where current tools fall short for real, paying companies — validated opportunities from your future competitors' own customers.

### Upwork
Every job posting is a business paying to solve a problem manually. BigIdeasDB finds the repetitive paid tasks that can be **automated into SaaS products** — the clearest willingness-to-pay signal there is. 📺 [Upwork Analysis tutorial](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=U6HX95A4GHI)

### Apple App Store & Google Play
Negative app reviews tell you exactly what mobile users want and aren't getting. Search any keyword to see how many apps and real reviews sit behind it, then drill into AI analysis for every app. 📺 [App Store Analysis tutorial](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=D9DUf6rQ8eg)

### Product Hunt
Track launches and trends to see which categories are heating up, what positioning works, and where the crowded spaces are — before you commit.

### Stripe, TrustMRR, Acquire.com & Y Combinator
Complaints tell you what to build; **revenue data tells you what it's worth.** BigIdeasDB layers in verified revenue from Stripe and TrustMRR, acquisition listings from Acquire.com, and portfolio data from Y Combinator — so every opportunity comes with proof that money is already moving. 📺 [TrustMRR verified revenue data](https://x.com/om_patel5/status/2026521517102612502)

## ❓ Frequently Asked Questions

**What is BigIdeasDB?**
BigIdeasDB is an AI-powered product-research and idea-validation platform that analyzes 1M+ real user complaints across Reddit, G2, Capterra, Upwork, and the app stores to help founders discover validated startup ideas, then plan and build them. It's free to start.

**How is BigIdeasDB different from doing my own research?**
It aggregates and AI-scores 1M+ data points across 10 platforms instantly. What would take weeks of manual scrolling is available in seconds — every problem carrying severity, frequency, and market-gap scores, plus a direct link to the original source.

**What is a "validated" startup idea?**
An idea tied to a documented, recurring, high-severity complaint that people already pay (badly) to solve — the opposite of a brainstormed guess. BigIdeasDB surfaces these bottom-up, from real evidence.

**Is BigIdeasDB a GummySearch or Reddit API alternative?**
Yes. The BigIdeasDB MCP server is a drop-in Reddit API / PRAW alternative — no OAuth, no API keys — and covers far more than Reddit (G2, Capterra, App Stores, Upwork, revenue, funding). GummySearch was Reddit-only and is shutting down in November 2026.

**How much does it cost?**
There's a free tier (tools, calculators, idea generator, limited browsing). Paid plans start at $99 lifetime, with full Pro access at $349 lifetime or $45/month — databases, hosted MCP, Revenue Intelligence, BuildHub, and priority support.

**Is the data current?**
Yes. Databases update continuously, TrustMRR revenue is scraped daily, and Reddit pipelines run up to 5× per day.
